## 🧠 LEBS — Low Entropy Business Systems

> Same people. Same time. Optimal allocation. Optimal outcome.

---

### Definition

**LEBS (Low Entropy Business Systems)** is a business operating model focused on improving how organizations convert effort into value.

It does this by:
- reducing distortion  
- improving coherence  
- aligning signals with facts and circumstances  

The result is a system that compounds value more effectively over time.

---

## Context

> **When systems optimize for attention, distortion increases.  
When systems optimize for coherence, efficiency improves.**

> **The Attention Economy prioritizes engagement.  
The next competitive advantage is coherence.**

> **As attention becomes saturated, coherence becomes scarce.  
Scarcity creates opportunity.**

---

## Core Principle

> **Distortion Load reduces the rate of value compounding.  
Correction Velocity determines how quickly distortion accumulates or is resolved.**

---

## Why LEBS Exists

Distortion is a natural byproduct of operational dynamics.

As organizations operate:
- incentives shift  
- signals degrade  
- decisions lag  
- coordination increases  

This leads to a consistent pattern:

> **A portion of organizational capacity is applied to work that does not maximize value creation.**

This is not a function of individual intent.  
It is a function of system behavior.

---

## The Hidden Constraint

LEBS introduces a practical way to observe this:

### **Distortion Load (DL)**  
The portion of organizational capacity consumed by misalignment between signals, incentives, and facts and circumstances.

### **Correction Velocity (CV)**  
The speed at which an organization detects and resolves distortion.

---

## System Dynamic

- Distortion emerges in all systems  
- If not corrected, it accumulates  
- Accumulated distortion reduces effective capacity  
- Reduced capacity lowers return on capital  
- Lower return reduces compounding  

Conversely:

- Faster correction limits accumulation  
- Lower distortion improves capacity allocation  
- Better allocation improves financial outcomes  
- Improved outcomes compound over time  

---

## Compounding Model

g_effective = g_base − (α × DL) + (β × CV)

## Key Insight

> **Small improvements in system efficiency can create large differences in compounding outcomes.**

## Financial Interpretation

LEBS does not depend on:
- additional effort  
- increased headcount  
- or cost reduction  

It focuses on:

> **better utilization of existing capacity**

This improves:
- revenue per employee  
- margin efficiency  
- speed of execution  
- long-term compounding
